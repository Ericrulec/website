Passwords in a modern era

November 22, 2023 · 5 min read · by Erik Jensson



Our passwords have never been more exposed to bad actors than as of writing. More and more passwords are leaking out of servers, with more people treating passwords like a public joke, than the secret passage the password is supposed to be.

Weak passwords are the main reason people get hacked. And sometimes it can be difficult to differentiate between good and bad passwords. I will not go into much details about what makes password good or bad, but the main gist is to make it sufficiently long and random. But this is also kind of the problem. I have only so much space in my head, memorizing all these scrambled hyroglyphs is going to bite me in the ass sooner or later. So either I write down some of them on a piece of paper or digitally on a notepad. Or I could do the sane thing and store them via a password manager.
